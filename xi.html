Xanthippus; de viris illustribus 40; Livy XXVIII 43.19, periochae 18; Cicero de officiis III 99; Diodorus Siculus XXIII 14-16b; Appian Libyke 3c; Zonaras (Cassius Dio XI) VIII 13; Valerius Maximus I 1.14, 9.6 externi 1d; L. Annaeus Florus I 18.23; Vegetius epitome rei militaris 3, preface; Eutropius II 21.4; Silius Italicus II [305], 434, IV 357, 361, VI [327-8], 681-3e, IX 67; Bevan, House of Ptolemy 194-196 (St. Jerome on Daniel xi 7-9): is the "Xanthippus" in Ptolemy III's army the Spartan Xanthippus?
  • A Spartiateb "of the Laconian agoge"a
  • Sent by the Spartan government at the request of the Carthaginians(?)c, he commanded a Carthaginian army in the First Punic War. He defeated a Roman consular army and captured its commander, Regulus, 255 BC. Shortly after his victory he sailed for homea.
  • Tradition had it that he was murdered by the Carthaginiansde.
  • Xanthippos is a unique name Spartan name. Is it found in the family of Leotychidas II because of his friendship with Xanthippos the father of Pericles?
